# Attention Is All You Need

**Attention Is All You Need** is a landmark research paper published by Vaswani et al. from Google Research in June 2017, which introduced the **Transformer** architecture. In the context of this wiki, it serves as one of two foundational sources and is the primary entity that catalyzed the modern large language model (LLM) revolution. The paper's central innovation was demonstrating that a neural network architecture relying *solely* on self-attention mechanisms—eliminating all recurrent and convolutional layers—could achieve state-of-the-art performance in machine translation tasks while being significantly more parallelizable and faster to train. This architectural choice addressed key limitations of prior sequence-to-sequence models, particularly their sequential processing constraints. The Transformer introduced key components including multi-head self-attention, positional encodings, and the encoder-decoder structure that later became the blueprint for nearly all subsequent LLMs. The paper's significance is underscored by its influence across the entire wiki knowledge graph: it defines the core technology underpinning [[intro-to-llms]] and establishes the technical lineage for all transformer-based systems discussed in the synthesis. Key actions associated with this entity include implementing the architecture for translation tasks, using its design patterns for scaling language models, and serving as the essential reference point for understanding modern NLP breakthroughs. The entity is also linked to [[Vaswani]] (the lead author) and [[Google]] (the research institution) through citations in its source material.
