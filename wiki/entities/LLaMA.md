# LLaMA

LLaMA (Large Language Model Meta AI) is a family of large language models developed by Meta, designed as a research-focused alternative to the proprietary, closed-source models created by organizations like [[OpenAI]] (e.g., [[GPT]]) and [[Google]] (e.g., [[BERT]]). Unlike many contemporary foundation models, LLaMA was released publicly with varying levels of openness—Meta initially distributed the model weights under a non-commercial license to academic researchers, and later released open-weight versions like LLaMA 2 and LLaMA 3 under a more permissive license. Architecturally, LLaMA is a decoder-only [[Transformer]]-based model, similar to the [[GPT]] family, using self-attention mechanisms as introduced in the foundational [[Attention Is All You Need]] paper. Its main significance lies in democratizing access to powerful LLM capabilities: by releasing the model weights, Meta enabled the broader research community, startups, and independent developers to fine-tune, study, and deploy high-performing language models without relying on expensive API calls or proprietary systems. This has spurred a wave of open-source innovation, including fine-tuned variants like Alpaca and Vicuna, and has directly challenged the dominance of closed-source foundation models by proving that open models can achieve competitive performance on a wide range of natural language tasks.
