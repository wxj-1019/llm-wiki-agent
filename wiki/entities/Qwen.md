# Qwen

Qwen is a family of large language models developed by Alibaba Cloud, emerging as a key player in the 2024 LLM landscape. In the context of Simon Willison's "Things we learned about LLMs in 2024" source, Qwen is significant as one of the model series that contributed to the "breaking of the GPT-4 barrier" — meaning that by the end of 2024, over 70 models from 18 different organizations, including Alibaba's Qwen series, surpassed the performance of the original GPT-4 from March 2023. Qwen models are particularly notable for being open-source (or open-weight), helping to drive down costs and increase accessibility in the LLM ecosystem. The Qwen family includes various sizes (e.g., Qwen 2.5, Qwen 7B, 14B, 32B, 72B) and multimodal variants capable of processing text, images, and sometimes audio or video. In Willison's review, Qwen is implicitly referenced as part of the broader trend where Chinese AI labs made significant contributions to the open-weight model landscape, alongside competitors like DeepSeek and Yi; however, unlike DeepSeek R1, Qwen was not specifically highlighted for inference-scaling reasoning capabilities in this particular source. The model family's release cadence and performance improvements throughout 2024 exemplify the accelerating pace of open-weight model development and the democratization of advanced LLM capabilities.
