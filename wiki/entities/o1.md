# o1

`o1` refers to OpenAI’s series of large language models introduced in 2024 that pioneer a new paradigm known as **inference-scaling** (or "test-time compute") reasoning. Unlike previous models (such as GPT-4) which generate responses in a single forward pass, o1 models are designed to “think” before responding by internally generating chains of reasoning, self-correcting, and exploring multiple solution paths during inference. This approach dramatically improves performance on complex mathematical, scientific, and coding tasks—at the cost of significantly higher computational overhead and latency. In the context of this wiki, o1 is most notable for representing a major architectural breakthrough that breaks the earlier scaling laws (which focused solely on pre-training compute), and for inspiring a wave of open-source imitators such as [[DeepSeekR1]]. The model’s emergence is frequently cited alongside DeepSeek R1 as a key milestone in 2024’s LLM landscape, and its release triggered widespread interest in inference-time optimization techniques, chain-of-thought prompting, and the trade-offs between raw intelligence and practical usability.
