# Claude

Claude is a family of large language models (LLMs) developed by Anthropic, positioned as a key competitor in the post-GPT-4 LLM landscape. In the context of 2024's rapid model proliferation, Claude represents a significant alternative to OpenAI's GPT-4 family and a prominent example of the "GPT-4 barrier being broken," as multiple Claude variants (including Claude 3 Opus, Sonnet, and Haiku) consistently ranked among the 70+ models that surpassed the original GPT-4's performance by the end of 2024. Claude is particularly noted for its emphasis on safety and constitutional AI training, its strong performance on coding and reasoning benchmarks, and its role in driving down inference costs across the industry. The model series spans a range of tiers from the lightweight, cost-efficient Haiku to the flagship Opus model, reflecting the broader 2024 trend of model specialization and price segmentation. Claude's release cadence and capabilities are frequently cited alongside other frontier models like Google's Gemini and Meta's Llama series as evidence of the democratization of high-quality AI capabilities.
