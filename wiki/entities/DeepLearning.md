# DeepLearning

DeepLearning refers to a subset of machine learning that utilizes multi-layered artificial neural networks to model complex patterns and representations in data. In the context of this wiki, DeepLearning is significant because it forms the foundational technique for advanced artificial intelligence systems, enabling capabilities such as image recognition, natural language processing, and autonomous decision-making. Its main significance lies in its ability to automatically learn hierarchical features from raw data, obviating the need for manual feature engineering, and its capacity to scale with increased computational power and data volume. Key actions and associations related to DeepLearning include the training of deep neural networks via backpropagation and gradient descent, the use of specialized hardware like GPUs and TPUs for efficient computation, and the application of architectures such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs), recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and transformers. In this wiki, DeepLearning is often linked to entities like specific frameworks (e.g., TensorFlow, PyTorch), associated techniques (e.g., transfer learning, regularization), and practical applications in domains ranging from healthcare to autonomous systems.
