# T5

T5, or Text-to-Text Transfer Transformer, is a family of encoder-decoder models developed by [[Google]]. Its defining innovation is reformulating all natural language processing (NLP) tasks—including translation, summarization, question answering, and classification—into a unified text-to-text framework, where both the input and output are always text strings. This allows a single model architecture and training objective to be applied across diverse tasks by simply prefixing the input with a task-specific cue (e.g., “translate English to German: …”). T5 is a key example of a foundation model built upon the [[Transformer]] architecture, leveraging the [[AttentionMechanism|self-attention]] mechanism introduced in the seminal paper [[Attention Is All You Need]]. The model demonstrated that a unified approach, combined with a large-scale “Colossal Clean Crawled Corpus” (C4), could achieve state-of-the-art performance on many benchmarks, and its design philosophy has influenced subsequent large language models by emphasizing the importance of transfer learning and task-agnostic pretraining.
