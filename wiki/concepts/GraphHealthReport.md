## Summary

The graph health report is a structured analysis generated by the [[BuildGraphTool|Build Graph Tool]] when run with `--report`. It evaluates the graph's structural health across several dimensions: edge/node ratio (ideal 1.5-3.0), orphan percentage (nodes with zero connections), number of communities, link density, and identification of special nodes like god nodes (hub pages with degree > μ+2σ), fragile bridges (community pairs connected by a single edge), and phantom hubs (missing pages referenced by 2+ existing pages that should be created). Saved to `graph/graph-report.md` with `--save`.

## Key Claims

- Detects orphan nodes for potential content linking improvements
- Identifies god nodes that may be overloaded (too many incoming links)
- Finds fragile bridges where graph fragmentation is a risk
- Suggests page creation candidates via phantom hub detection
