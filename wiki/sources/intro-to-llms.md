## Summary
A comprehensive overview of how [[LargeLanguageModels]] work, from tokenization to autoregressive generation, covering [[GPT]], [[BERT]], and modern architectures.

## Key Claims
- LLMs are fundamentally next-token predictors trained on vast corpora.
- Scaling laws predict that model capability improves predictably with compute, data, and parameters.
- Emergent abilities appear abruptly at certain scale thresholds.

## Key Quotes
> "The surprising effectiveness of simply predicting the next word at scale has reshaped our understanding of intelligence."

## Connections
- [[GPT]] — decoder-only architecture
- [[BERT]] — encoder-only architecture
- [[Transformer]] — foundational architecture
- [[OpenAI]] — leading research lab
- [[AttentionMechanism]] — core mechanism

## Contradictions
- Some researchers argue emergent abilities are a metric artifact rather than a fundamental property.
